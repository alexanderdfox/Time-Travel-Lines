# Time Travel Lines - All 7 Sins
## Use Cases, Pros & Cons

Highly modular OpenSCAD parametric models inspired by the Seven Deadly Sins.

| Sin       | Design Name                  | Visual Description                          | Potential Use Cases                                                                 | Pros                                      | Cons                                      |
|-----------|------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| **Pride** | Clean Infinite Cylinder     | Straight, simple, perfectly uniform tube   | Lamp shades, minimalist vases, structural pipes, clean architectural columns, pen holders, telescope tubes | Extremely simple & reliable<br>Fast to render/print<br>Strong & uniform walls<br>Easy to modify | Least visually interesting<br>Can feel "boring" compared to others |
| **Envy**  | Concentric Flared Shells    | Nested, scaling, slightly twisted shells   | Decorative vases, artistic lamps, nested storage containers, jewelry displays, sculptural pillars | Beautiful layered effect<br>Good visual depth<br>Interesting light play through gaps | More complex geometry<br>Higher material use<br>Potential print supports needed |
| **Greed** | Corrugated Conduit          | Wavy, periodically varying radius          | Flexible ducting, cable management sleeves, garden hoses, acoustic dampeners, flexible grips | Looks industrial/cool<br>Good flexibility in some orientations<br>Visually dynamic | Slightly weaker at narrow points<br>More surface area (harder to clean) |
| **Wrath** | Chaotic Waving Tube         | Wildly offset, turbulent waving path       | Abstract sculptures, wild artistic lamps, organic-looking conduits, dynamic show pieces | Extremely striking & energetic<br>Great conversation piece<br>Very organic feel | Harder to print (overhangs)<br>Structurally weaker<br>More difficult to fit in tight spaces |
| **Sloth** | Interlocking Helical Cage   | Central tube + helical struts + rings      | Lightweight structural columns, plant trellises, decorative cages, artistic bird feeders, lamp frames | Very strong yet lightweight<br>Excellent ventilation<br>Unique mechanical aesthetic | More complex assembly if printed in parts<br>Longer print time |
| **Gluttony** | Alternating Hourglass     | Repeated bulging & constricting segments   | Hourglass-style vases, segmented storage, flexible connectors, decorative bottles, sound diffusers | Very rhythmic & pleasing pattern<br>Good for showing off filament changes<br>Strong visual rhythm | Potential weak points at narrow waists<br>Higher material variation stress |
| **Lust**  | Matrix / Finned Column      | Core tube + radial fins + grid layers      | Heat sinks, technical columns, futuristic lamp bases, mechanical sculptures, cable organizers | Excellent surface area (great for cooling)<br>Very "tech" / cyberpunk look<br>Strong in multiple axes | Most complex geometry<br>Longest print time<br>Requires good bed adhesion |

### Quick Recommendations

- **Best for beginners / fast prints**: **Pride**
- **Best for decoration / art**: **Envy**, **Wrath**, or **Lust**
- **Best structural / functional**: **Sloth** or **Lust**
- **Most unique / eye-catching**: **Wrath** or **Lust**
- **Best for vases / containers**: **Gluttony** or **Envy**
