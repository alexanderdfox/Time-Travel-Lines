7 Deadly Sins of Time Travel

Hover over any chronological hazard to examine its paradoxical risk.

1. Pride (Hubris of the Straight Line)

The Absolute Determinism Fallacy Believing you can travel back over a rigid, single line and change an event without rewriting your own motivations for going back in the first place.

2. Envy (The Fractured Splinter)

The Multiverse Splinter Envying a better reality and altering the past, only to spawn a new branch. You remain trapped in your original timeline, having saved a version of "you" that isn't you.

3. Greed (The Endless Bootstrap)

The Ontological Loop Stealing ideas or artifacts from the future to invent them in the past. Information endlessly loops with no true origin point, defying entropy.

4. Wrath (The Butterfly Echo)

The Amplified Chaos Wave Striking back at a past enemy or event, sending catastrophic ripples forward that radically deform the future in unpredictable, oscillating waves.

5. Sloth (The Causal Grandfather Knot)

The Self-Canceling Snarl Failing to do your baseline temporal maintenance, creating a paradox where you delete your own ancestry and tie reality into an unresolvable logical knot.

6. Gluttony (The Ouroboros Feast)

The Infinity Paradox Consuming your own future resources to feed your past self, forcing reality to fold back into a figure-eight loop that eats its own tail forever.

7. Lust (The Tangled Mesh)

The Reality Overlap Grid An obsession with manipulating multiple timelines at once, leading to overlapping dimensions that collapse into an unstable matrix.
